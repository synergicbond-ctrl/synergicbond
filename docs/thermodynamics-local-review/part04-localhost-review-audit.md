## Scientific corrections
- Constant-pressure process need not be an open-system process
- State variables do change during a reversible process; they remain well-defined
- Quasi-static is necessary but not sufficient for reversibility
- Every adiabatic process is not isentropic
- A complete cycle has net ΔSsystem = 0 but is not necessarily isentropic throughout
- Reversibility must restore both system and surroundings
- Intermediate equilibrium variables may be undefined during a strongly irreversible process
- Open-vessel examples are approximately isobaric because atmospheric pressure is nearly constant
- Chemistry sign convention used consistently for cyclic work
- “Reversible never reaches completion” removed as scientifically incorrect
