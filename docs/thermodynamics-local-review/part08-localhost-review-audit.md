## Primary handwritten sequence coverage
- Laws of Thermodynamics heading
- Zeroth Law statement:
  - if two systems are separately in thermal equilibrium with a third,
    they are in thermal equilibrium with each other
- First Law based on conservation of energy
- Energy of system and surroundings together remains constant
- Energy of the universe remains constant
- Initial internal energy U1
- Heat q supplied to the system
- Work w done on the system
- Final internal energy U2
- U2 = U1 + q + w
- ΔU = q + w
- Differential form dU = δq + δw
- With pressure–volume work:
  dU = δq − Pext dV
- Rearranged form:
  δq = dU + Pext dV
- Closed-system and pressure–volume-work scope
- Heat and work as equivalent ways of changing internal energy

## Scientific corrections and safeguards
- Conservation applies to system plus surroundings, not necessarily the system alone
- ΔU = q + w is presented with its closed-system and reference-frame assumptions
- Open systems require mass-flow energy terms
- Constant volume removes only pressure–volume work
- ΔU = 0 does not force q = 0 and w = 0 separately
- Isolated systems may undergo internal energy conversions
- Zeroth Law is not confused with direction of heat flow
- First Law is not used as a spontaneity criterion
- Differential notation distinguishes dU from δq and δw
- Reversible-pressure substitution Pext → P is used only in the reversible limit
